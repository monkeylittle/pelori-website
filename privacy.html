Pelori Privacy Policy

⚠️ This is a starter draft intended as a working baseline. Have a lawyer review and tailor it to your jurisdiction before publishing the URL referenced from the App Store / Play Store listing.

Last updated: TBD

Pelori ("we", "us") provides a push-to-talk (PTT) and ride-coordination app for cyclists. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, and the controls you have.

Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Account profile — email, first/family name, date of birth (used to verify the 16+ minimum age), country, and an optional profile picture.
  • Ride content — ride name and description, schedules, meeting points, route polylines you import (GPX or from Strava), and the membership of each ride.
  • Voice transmissions — captured live through LiveKit while you are connected to a ride; not recorded or stored by Pelori.
  • Feedback — text you submit through the in-app feedback form, along with the app version and platform sent automatically.

Information collected automatically

  • Location — when you toggle "share location" on a ride, your device's coordinates, heading, and speed are broadcast to other participants of that ride for the duration of the ride. Pelori does not persist live-location history server-side; the data lives in participant attributes inside LiveKit and is gone when you leave.
  • Diagnostic information — anonymised crash and performance reports via Sentry. We do not attach request bodies or PII to these reports.

Third-party integrations

  • Strava — if you connect Strava, we receive an OAuth refresh/access token and basic athlete name to import your routes. We do not access your activities. Disconnecting Strava in profile settings revokes our token.

How we use your information

  • Operate the service: authenticate you, deliver invitations, broadcast voice and location to ride participants, render rides on maps.
  • Enforce the 16+ age requirement.
  • Investigate bugs and improve the app via crash reports and feedback.

We do not sell your data or use it for advertising.

Where data is stored

  • AWS (London region, eu-west-2 by default) — Cognito for authentication, DynamoDB for ride and feedback data, S3 for profile pictures.
  • LiveKit Cloud — runtime voice and live-location channels.
  • Strava — only if you have connected Strava; data flow is from Strava to the app at your request.

Data retention

  • Account data: retained while your account is active. Deleting your account removes profile, rides you organise, memberships, Strava tokens, and avatar files (see Your rights below).
  • Feedback messages: retained indefinitely so we can act on them.
  • Crash diagnostics: retained per Sentry's default retention.

Your rights

  • Access / export — contact us at the address below and we will provide a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Deletion — open Profile → Delete account in the app. Your Cognito user, every ride you organise (and its members), all your memberships, your Strava connection, and your avatar files are permanently removed. Deletion is immediate and not recoverable.
  • Correction — edit your profile in the app, or contact us if a field is not directly editable.
  • Withdraw consent — toggle "share location" off, disconnect Strava, or delete your account at any time.

Children

Pelori is for users aged 16 and older. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16. If we learn we have, we delete the account.

Security

We use HTTPS for all transport, JWT-authenticated APIs, and least- privilege IAM roles. We rotate third-party credentials via AWS Secrets Manager. No system is perfectly secure, so use a strong unique password.

Changes

We will update the "Last updated" date when this policy changes. Major changes are surfaced in-app the next time you sign in.
